## PROGRAM-SPECIFIC COPY

### Health Outreach Programme (HOP):
> CPI's Health Outreach Programme (HOP) deploys trained Community Health Workers (CHWs) across refugee camps in Cox's Bazar to provide household-level health services, education, and referrals. CHWs serve as the first point of contact for community members, bridging the gap between the community and formal health services.

### Health Post (HP) Program:
> CPI's Health Post provides static primary health care services to Rohingya refugees in Cox's Bazar, serving up to 200 patients daily. Services include general outpatient care, chronic disease management, screening programs, and referral to higher-level facilities.

### HCV (Hepatitis C) Program:
> CPI Bangladesh's Hepatitis C program follows the WHO 90-90-90 cascade of care approach: testing 90% of the estimated HCV-positive population, linking 90% of those diagnosed to treatment, and achieving sustained virological response (SVR) in 90% of treated patients. Community Health Workers support mobilization, follow-up, and adherence within the refugee camp setting.

### CVD/NCD Program:
> Non-communicable diseases including hypertension and diabetes are a growing burden among Rohingya refugees. CPI's CVD/NCD program provides systematic screening, diagnosis, treatment, and follow-up care, helping patients manage chronic conditions and reduce risk of life-threatening complications.

### WASH Program:
> CPI Bangladesh builds and maintains water wells and water networks, toilet and washing facilities, and supports hygiene promotion through trained community health and hygiene workers — ensuring Rohingya refugees have access to safe water and sanitation in one of the world's most densely populated humanitarian settings.

### Emergency Response:
> CPI Bangladesh trains and equips community-based disaster response teams to respond to fires, floods, and cyclones — the most frequent emergencies in Cox's Bazar camps. Rapid response saves lives and supports community recovery in the critical hours after a disaster.

## ROHINGYA CRISIS CONTEXT COPY

### Background paragraph:
> More than 970,000 Rohingya refugees from Myanmar shelter in Cox's Bazar District, Bangladesh — making it one of the world's largest concentrations of displaced people. More than half are children. Since the mass displacement of August 2017, when more than 740,000 Rohingya fled violence in Myanmar's Rakhine State, CPI has worked alongside these communities to provide essential services and strengthen their resilience.

### Crisis severity (current):
> Rohingya refugees in Cox's Bazar face worsening conditions: dramatic aid cuts, rising population density, increasing food insecurity, and growing vulnerability to climate events. The World Food Programme reduced monthly rations to $6 per person in early 2025. As resources shrink, community-level health services become even more critical.

### Host community paragraph:
> With Rohingya refugees comprising approximately one-third of Cox's Bazar District's total population, Bangladeshi host communities also face mounting development pressures. CPI supports sustainable water, sanitation, and livelihood initiatives that benefit both refugees and their Bangladeshi neighbors, promoting peaceful coexistence and shared resilience.
