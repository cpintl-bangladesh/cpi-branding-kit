## TONE OF VOICE PRINCIPLES

### 1. EMPOWERING
**What it means:** Center the agency, resilience, and capacity of the communities we serve. Never portray communities as passive recipients or victims.

**Do write:**
- *"Rohingya community health workers made over 200,000 household visits…"*
- *"Women like Laili Akter are now leading water access solutions in their village…"*
- *"Communities in Cox's Bazar are building their own health systems…"*

**Don't write:**
- *"We help helpless refugees…"*
- *"Vulnerable people who depend entirely on our charity…"*
- *"We rescued the community from…"*

### 2. INSPIRING
**What it means:** Give readers a sense of hope and possibility. Connect them to the real impact of their support or their work.

**Do write:**
- *"Your support sustains health post services reaching up to 200 refugees daily."*
- *"Together, we are building systems that will serve communities long into the future."*

**Don't write:**
- *"Without us, everything would collapse."*
- *"The situation is hopeless without intervention."*

### 3. INCLUSIVE & RESPECTFUL
**What it means:** Uphold non-discrimination and dignity in all language. Every person is an individual, not a statistic or stereotype.

**Do write:**
- *"Rohingya refugees"* not *"the Rohingya"* (when referring to specific individuals)
- *"communities affected by conflict"* not *"war-torn populations"*
- Use real names in stories when consent is given

**Don't write:**
- Dehumanizing or generalizing language
- Labels that strip individual identity

### 4. ACCESSIBLE
**What it means:** Write in plain English. Explain technical terms. Avoid jargon. Be direct.

**Preferred vocabulary:**
- *"health care"* not *"medical intervention delivery"*
- *"community health workers"* not *"CHW cadres"* (unless in technical reports)
- *"Rohingya refugee camps"* not *"informal settlements for displaced persons"*

### 5. APPROPRIATE
**What it means:** Match your language register to your audience and context.
- **Donor communications:** Warm, personal, story-driven, impact-focused
- **Technical reports:** Precise, evidence-based, sector-standard terminology acceptable
- **Social media:** Concise, engaging, accessible, with clear call to action
- **Official letters:** Formal, professional, brief
- **Program materials (field):** Plain, community-level language; translated where needed

## BRAND VALUES IN LANGUAGE

CPI's values should be reflected in how we write:

| Value | Language Implication |
|-------|---------------------|
| Partnership | Use "with" not "for" — *"We work with communities"* |
| Local leadership | Credit local staff and partners by name and role |
| Transparency | Be specific about numbers, outcomes, challenges |
| Accountability | Acknowledge when things are difficult; be honest |
| Non-discrimination | Equal weight across genders, ethnicities, communities |
| Evidence-based | Ground claims in data; cite sources |

## WORDS & PHRASES TO USE / AVOID

### ✅ USE
- empowers / empowering / empowerment
- communities
- local leaders / local organizations / local partners
- essential services / essential needs
- resilient / resilience
- sustainable
- equitable access
- community-led
- partnership of equals
- complex contexts / fragile settings
- conflict-affected / poverty-affected
- Rohingya refugees (Bangladesh context)

### ❌ AVOID
- beneficiaries (use "communities" or specific terms like "refugees", "patients")
- the poor / the needy (dehumanizing)
- charity / charitable handout
- we saved / we rescued (centers CPI over community)
- remote/backward/undeveloped (use "underserved" or "hard-to-reach")
- illegal immigrants (use "refugees" or "displaced persons")
- tribal (use specific ethnic group names)
