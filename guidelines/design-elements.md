# CPI Design Elements Guide
## Community Partners International — Visual Design System

**Source:** CPI Brand Identity & Standards Designer's Guide (Official)

---

## KEY SHAPES (Brand Design Elements)

CPI uses four distinct key shapes to frame images and text. These add visual character and consistency across all materials.

### Shape 1 — Rectangle with Rounded Top Corner
- A rectangle where only the **top corners are rounded** (radius: ~10–15% of height)
- Used as an **overlay box on top of an image** — typically in CPI Red or CPI Purple
- Contains: title text or logo
- Common on: report covers, posters, banners, social cards

**How to build it:**
```
Rectangle → apply border-radius to top-left and top-right corners only
Fill: CPI Red #D91E4D or CPI Purple #41273B
Text inside: White, Raleway Bold/Semi-Bold
Position: Lower-left or lower-center of an image
```

### Shape 2 — Image in Key Shape (Title + Photo Layout)
- Title text sits beside or overlaps a photo that is framed/clipped into a CPI key shape
- The shape acts as a photo "window" with soft geometric edges
- Used on: covers, feature sections, impact story layouts

**Variations:**
- Diagonal split: image on left, colored panel on right
- Rounded-corner card: image fully inside a rounded rectangle

### Shape 3 — Image in Key Shape on Side (Left or Right)
- Photo is placed in the key shape and positioned to the left or right of the layout
- Text content fills the opposite side
- Used on: two-column layouts, name cards, brochure panels

### Shape 4 — Stroke Line + Rounded Photo
- A **thin stroke line in CPI Red `#D91E4D`** runs vertically or diagonally
- A photo appears in a **rounded/circular or rounded-rectangle shape** adjacent to the line
- Used on: pull quotes, profile features, testimonials

**Stroke specs:**
- Weight: 2–4pt (print) / 2–3px (digital)
- Color: CPI Red `#D91E4D`
- Style: Solid, no dashes

## WATERMARK USAGE

The CPI butterfly/leaf logo mark can be used as a watermark background element.

### Rules:
- **Placement:** Right side of the layout ONLY (bottom right preferred)
- **Size:** Flexible — can be very large (bleed off edge) or moderate
- **Color on white backgrounds:** CPI Mid Grey `#948794` at **46% opacity**
- **Color on CPI brand color backgrounds:** CPI Mid Grey `#948794` at **46% opacity** or full opacity grey in Multiply blend mode
- **Never:** Left side, center, or on busy photographic areas
- **Never:** Full color butterfly mark as watermark

## OVERLAY COLORS ON PHOTOS

CPI brand colors can be used as semi-transparent overlays on photographs to create unified visuals.

### Primary Overlay:
- CPI Red `#D91E4D` at **60–80% opacity** in Multiply or Color blend mode
- Creates a bold red-tinted photograph effect

### Secondary Overlays:
- CPI Purple `#41273B` at 60–80% opacity
- CPI Blue `#4298B5` at 60–80% opacity
- CPI Mid Grey `#948794` at 60–80% opacity

### Application:
- Place a solid color fill layer above the photograph
- Set blend mode to **Multiply** (Photoshop/Illustrator/Figma)
- Adjust opacity to 60–80% for best result
- Use for full-bleed cover images, social media hero images, section banners

## ICON SYSTEM

CPI uses circular icons in two styles:

### Style A — Non-Solid Fill (Outline icons)
- White circle outline with CPI Red icon inside on white/light backgrounds
- Or: CPI Red circle outline with white icon
- **Border:** 2pt CPI Red stroke circle

### Style B — Solid Fill Icons
- Filled CPI Red circle with white icon inside
- Used on colored backgrounds or dark panels

### Official CPI Icon Set (from brand guide):
1. **Community/Partnership icon** — circle of people/hands
2. **Family/Maternal icon** — family group symbol
3. **Health/Care icon** — cupped hands holding plant/heart
4. **Shelter/Home icon** — house with care symbol

### Additional Recommended Icons for Program Use:
- 🏥 Health facility
- 💧 WASH / Water
- 🤱 Maternal & newborn
- 📊 M&E / Data
- 🌱 Sustainable Development
- 🚨 Humanitarian Response
- 👩‍⚕️ Community Health Worker
- 🔬 Research

> Use icons from consistent sets (e.g., Material Design, Feather Icons) and apply CPI Red fill. Avoid cartoon or clipart styles.

## PHOTOGRAPHY GUIDELINES

### Philosophy
> *"We avoid photographs that depict individuals and communities as passive recipients. Instead, we use photographs that show vibrant and diverse communities, actively engaged and empowered in leading the solutions to their challenges."*

### Photo DO's:
- ✅ Show people actively engaged — working, learning, caring, leading
- ✅ Capture genuine expressions of dignity, resilience, and hope
- ✅ Include community health workers and local staff in action
- ✅ Show community spaces, clinics, water points, training sessions
- ✅ Include a diversity of ages, genders, and roles
- ✅ Photograph in natural light where possible
- ✅ Get appropriate consent/photo release for all subjects

### Photo DON'Ts:
- ❌ Do NOT show patients in clinical distress without consent
- ❌ Do NOT depict communities as helpless, defeated, or pitiful
- ❌ Do NOT use images that could compromise individual dignity
- ❌ Do NOT show children's faces without guardian consent
- ❌ Do NOT use stock photo clichés (single emaciated child, dramatic suffering)
- ❌ Do NOT over-expose Western staff/visitors at the expense of local leaders

### Photo Treatment Options:
1. **Full color, natural** — standard use for reports, stories
2. **CPI color overlay** — for headers, covers, social media (see Overlay Colors)
3. **Black & white** — for archival or historical context
4. **Clipped in key shape** — for feature layouts (see Key Shapes)
5. **With caption** — always credit photographer (e.g., *Photo: Md. Dipu/CPI*)

## LAYOUT COMPOSITION PRINCIPLES

### Grid System:
- **Print (A4):** 12-column grid, 10mm margins, 5mm gutters
- **Print (Letter US):** 12-column grid, 1-inch margins, 0.2-inch gutters
- **Digital (screen):** 12-column grid, responsive breakpoints
- **Social Media:** 3×3 or 2×2 safe zone composition

### Layout Types:
1. **Full-bleed cover** — photo fills entire page, title overlay in key shape
2. **Split layout** — 40/60 or 50/50 image and text panels
3. **Text-heavy report** — single or double column, generous white space
4. **Infographic** — CPI color palette with icons and data callouts
5. **Story layout** — large photo + pull quote + body text

### White Space:
- Be generous — CPI materials should feel clean and accessible, not crowded
- Minimum 10mm margin on all printed pages
- Minimum 20px padding in digital layouts

## DIVIDER & SEPARATOR ELEMENTS

| Element | Specs |
|---------|-------|
| Horizontal rule (section divider) | 1pt, CPI Red `#D91E4D`, full width |
| Vertical rule (column separator or co-brand) | 1pt, CPI Mid Grey `#948794` |
| Dot separator | 4pt circle, CPI Red, for tagline or bulleted list |
| Section number | Raleway Light, large (60–80pt), CPI Red, very low opacity (10–15%) as background |

## CALLOUT BOX STYLES

### Stat Box / Key Figure:
```
Background: CPI Red #D91E4D
Number: Raleway Bold, 48–60pt, White
Label: Raleway Regular, 12pt, White
Border radius: 4–8px
Padding: 16–24px
```

### Pull Quote Box:
```
Left border: 4pt solid CPI Red
Background: CPI Light Grey #D0C4C5 at 30% opacity
Text: Raleway Semi-Bold, 14–16pt, CPI Purple
Attribution: Raleway Regular, 11pt, CPI Mid Grey
```

### Info/Note Box:
```
Background: CPI Blue #4298B5 at 15% opacity
Border-left: 3pt CPI Blue
Text: Arial/Raleway Regular, 11pt, CPI Black
```

### Warning/Critical Box:
```
Background: CPI Red #D91E4D at 10% opacity
Border-left: 3pt CPI Red
Text: Arial/Raleway Regular, 11pt, CPI Black
```
