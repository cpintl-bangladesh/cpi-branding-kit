## OPERATIONAL CONTEXT

### Population Served
- **Primary:** Rohingya refugee population — approximately 970,000+ refugees in 33–34 camps in Cox's Bazar District (UN estimate)
- **Secondary:** Bangladeshi host communities in Cox's Bazar District
- **Camp Focus:** Camp 1W (Blocks A & B), Camp 4 (Blocks D & F) and surrounding areas
- **Camp Size Context:** Kutupalong Refugee Camp (~23 camps combined) houses nearly 800,000 refugees — world's largest refugee camp
- **Population Density:** 47,000+ people/km² — among highest in the world

### CPI Bangladesh Operational Year
- Bangladesh operation launched: **2017** (following mass displacement from Myanmar)
- Scale: Since 2017, continuous health and humanitarian services

### Key Programs (Health)
1. **Health Outreach Programme (HOP)**
   - Community-based health services
   - Facility UID: HF-418 (anchor facility reference)
   - Outreach teams across designated blocks
   - Includes Mass Drug Administration (MDA) for scabies and other conditions

2. **Health Post (HP) Program**
   - Static facility serving up to 200 refugees daily
   - Clinical services including OPD, screening, referral

3. **Hepatitis C Virus (HCV) Program**
   - Cascade of care following WHO 90-90-90 targets
   - Screening → Diagnosis → Treatment → SVR monitoring
   - CHW-led community mobilization and follow-up

4. **CVD/NCD Program**
   - Cardiovascular Disease & Non-Communicable Disease management
   - Screening, diagnosis, treatment, follow-up

5. **WASH Services**
   - Water well construction and maintenance
   - Sanitation facility support
   - Hygiene promotion through CHW network

6. **Emergency Preparedness**
   - Community-based disaster response teams
   - Response to fires, floods, and cyclones

### Local Partner

## DATA & REPORTING CONTEXT

### Key Indicators (Health Programs):
**HCV Program (WHO 90-90-90 targets):**
1. % of estimated HCV+ patients tested
2. % of diagnosed patients linked to treatment
3. % of treated patients achieving SVR (Sustained Virological Response)
4. Total patients enrolled in care
5. HCV screening rate by camp/block

**CVD/NCD Program:**
1. Hypertension screening coverage
2. Diabetes screening coverage
3. Treatment enrollment rate
4. Blood pressure control rate (<140/90)
5. Medication adherence rate
6. Lost to follow-up rate

**CHW/Community Outreach:**
1. Total household visits per month
2. Referrals made to health post
3. Community education sessions conducted
4. CHW supervision completion rate
5. Health messaging coverage (% of target households)

### Reporting Frequency:
- **Weekly:** EWARS, activity counts
- **Monthly:** DHIS2, ActivityInfo, internal program reports
- **Quarterly:** Donor reports, Health Sector 3W/4W
- **Annually:** Annual Program Report, M&E assessment
